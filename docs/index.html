Biography

My interests lie anywhere I can combine my skills in the broad disciplines of math, computer science, and statistics to solve real-world issues. This is the most fulfilling path for me, as I enjoy seeing and using the vast connections between each area. Recently, I spent the summer doing research in numerical analysis, a field that heavily draws on both math and computer science. Working with Professor Will Mitchell, we developed a new numerical integration technique.

As a student, I strive to have exposure to as many ideas as possible. This helps me become a more well-rounded person and better adapted to take on new challenges. I have also embraced the role of peer-tutor. It is an opportunity for me to help other students, and further my own learning. If I can teach my peers these topics, then that means I will know them that much better. But most of all, I have the privilege to help educate my peers, which is something that I think is very meaningful and a great opportunity for me as an undergraduate.

Personal

At Macalester, a lot of my free time is devoted to the open ultimate frisbee team, Flat Earth. As a captain, I have a variety of roles, from managing team finances and fundraising, to long-term planning. Being ingrained in the team so deeply has been very rewarding. And playing frisbee is one of those things that never gets old.

I really love backpacking. My twin brother (also at Macalester) and I spend a week in the wilderness each summer, exploring new parks and places. Lately, we have checked Glacier National Park and Mt. Assiniboine Provincial Park in British Columbia off our bucket list. Staying active in ways that allow me to explore the outdoors is something that I look forward to every year, and backpacking is an activity that I can do for the rest of my life.

I also enjoy games. As far as board games go, Terraforming Mars is my go-to. It’s the most complex board game that I’ve found, which might be why I like it so much - there are more ways to gain an edge on my opponents. My goal is to enter a Terraforming Mars tournament, if such things exist. My other favorite game has to be Sudoku. It seems like such a simple game - place each digit 1-9 in each row, column, and sub-grid - and yet, I have found it to be very stimulating. I especially enjoy playing the many variants out there, including Chess, Arrow, and Thermal sudoku.
